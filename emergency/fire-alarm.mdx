# üî• Fire Alarm Procedures

> **Critical Safety Information:** These procedures are designed to teach real emergency response skills that could save lives in actual fire emergencies.

When the fire alarm sounds, every second counts. Follow these procedures exactly to ensure everyone's safety.

---

## üö® When the Fire Alarm Sounds

### Immediate Actions (First 10 seconds)

<Warning>
**STOP** what you are doing immediately. Do not finish conversations, collect belongings, or hesitate.
</Warning>

1. **Listen for the alarm type:**
   - **Continuous tone** = Fire evacuation required
   - **Intermittent tone** = Investigate and prepare
   - **Voice announcement** = Follow specific instructions

2. **Alert others around you:**
   - Call out "Fire alarm! Everyone evacuate!"
   - Help anyone who may not have heard the alarm
   - Do NOT use elevators

---

## üö™ Step-by-Step Evacuation

### Phase 1: Immediate Response (10-30 seconds)

1. **Leave everything behind**
   - Do not collect personal belongings
   - Do not save work or shut down computers
   - Safety is more important than possessions

2. **Check your immediate area**
   - Ensure no one is left behind
   - Help anyone who needs assistance
   - Close doors behind you (but do not lock them)

### Phase 2: Exit the Building (30 seconds - 3 minutes)

3. **Use the nearest safe exit**
   - **Primary exits:** Main entrances and stairwells
   - **Secondary exits:** Side doors and emergency exits
   - **Never use elevators** during a fire alarm

4. **Follow evacuation routes**
   - Stay low if you encounter smoke
   - Test doors with the back of your hand before opening
   - If a door is hot, find an alternate route

5. **Assist others when safe to do so**
   - Help mobility-impaired individuals
   - Guide younger students
   - Stay calm and encourage others

### Phase 3: Assembly (3-5 minutes)

6. **Go directly to your assembly point**
   - **Front Assembly Point:** Main parking lot
   - **Rear Assembly Point:** Athletic field  
   - **Side Assembly Point:** Side parking area
   - Stay at least 150 feet from the building

## üìç Assembly Point Procedures

### At the Assembly Point

<Info>
Assembly points are safe zones where we account for everyone and coordinate with emergency responders.
</Info>

1. **Report to your designated area**
   - Students: Line up by classroom/grade
   - Staff: Report to the staff assembly area
   - Visitors: Go to the visitor assembly area

2. **Stay in your assigned area**
   - Do not wander around
   - Do not leave the assembly point
   - Wait for official instructions

3. **Remain quiet and listen**
   - Staff will take attendance
   - Listen for important announcements
   - Emergency responders may need to communicate

### What NOT to Do at Assembly Points

‚ùå **Do not:**
- Re-enter the building for any reason
- Leave the assembly area without permission
- Use this time for socializing or playing
- Block emergency vehicle access
- Interfere with staff taking attendance

## üë®‚Äçüè´ Staff Responsibilities During Fire Emergencies

### Teachers and Classroom Staff

1. **Immediate classroom response:**
   - Direct all students to evacuate immediately
   - Grab class roster and emergency folder
   - Turn off lights and close (don't lock) door
   - Be the last to leave the classroom

2. **During evacuation:**
   - Lead students to the nearest safe exit
   - Keep students together as a group
   - Account for all students continuously
   - Assist students with special needs

3. **At the assembly point:**
   - Take attendance using class roster
   - Report missing students to emergency coordinator
   - Keep students calm and organized
   - Do not allow students to leave

### Administrative Staff

1. **Ensure all areas are evacuated**
2. **Coordinate with emergency responders**
3. **Manage communication with parents/media**
4. **Account for all staff and visitors**

## üö´ What NOT to Do During Fire Emergencies

### Critical Mistakes to Avoid

<Warning>
These actions can be dangerous or deadly during a real fire emergency:
</Warning>

‚ùå **Never:**
- Ignore or delay response to fire alarms
- Use elevators during evacuation
- Go back for personal belongings
- Hide in bathrooms or closets
- Open doors that feel hot
- Run or push others during evacuation
- Leave the building through windows (unless instructed)
- Re-enter the building until cleared by fire department

‚ùå **Do not treat this as a game:**
- Fire emergencies are serious life-or-death situations
- Inappropriate behavior during drills will result in consequences
- Real fires can develop in minutes - every second matters

## üìû Communication Protocols

### During the Emergency

1. **PA System Priority:**
   - Emergency announcements take priority
   - Listen carefully to all instructions
   - Follow the most recent instructions given

2. **Emergency Chat Guidelines:**
   - Use chat only for emergency information
   - Report emergencies or people needing help
   - Do not spam or use chat for non-emergency purposes

3. **External Communication:**
   - Only designated staff contact emergency services
   - Do not call 911 unless you are in immediate danger
   - Parents will be notified through official channels

---

## üîÑ Post-Emergency Procedures

### After Fire Department Clearance

1. **Wait for official all-clear**
   - Only fire department can declare building safe
   - Staff will announce when it's safe to return
   - Do not return until given explicit permission

2. **Organized return to building**
   - Return in organized groups
   - Follow staff instructions
   - Report any damage or hazards observed

3. **Debrief and assessment**
   - Participate in evacuation drill feedback
   - Report any problems or concerns
   - Review procedures for improvement

## üí° Fire Safety Tips

### Prevention

- Report fire hazards immediately
- Keep evacuation routes clear
- Know the location of fire extinguishers
- Understand different types of fire alarms

### During Real Fires

- **Smoke rises:** Stay low to avoid smoke inhalation
- **Test doors:** Hot doors indicate fire on the other side
- **Stop, drop, and roll:** If your clothes catch fire
- **Call 911:** Once you are safely outside
