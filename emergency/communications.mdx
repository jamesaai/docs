## üîä PA Announcement System Types

### Fire Emergency Announcements

**Standard Fire Alarm:**
- **Tone:** Continuous alarm bell/horn
- **Voice:** "Attention: Fire alarm activated. All students and staff evacuate the building immediately using the nearest exit."
- **Repetition:** Announcement repeats every 30 seconds

**Fire Investigation:**
- **Tone:** Intermittent beeping
- **Voice:** "Attention: Fire alarm system activated. Please standby for further instructions."
- **Action:** Prepare to evacuate but wait for additional instructions

### Lockdown Announcements

**Immediate Lockdown:**
- **Voice:** "Lockdown, lockdown, lockdown. Secure all areas immediately."
- **Repetition:** Announced 3 times quickly
- **Action:** Immediate lockdown procedures

**Secure Building:**
- **Voice:** "Attention: Secure the building. All exterior doors are to be locked. Continue with current activities."
- **Action:** Soft lockdown - secure perimeter but continue activities

### All-Clear Announcements

**Emergency Over:**
- **Voice:** "All clear. The emergency situation has ended. Resume normal activities."
- **Confirmation:** Announcement repeated 3 times
- **Staff Verification:** Confirmed by visual contact with known staff

- ‚ùå Social conversations or jokes
- ‚ùå Speculation about the emergency

### Chat Priority System

1. **EMERGENCY (Highest Priority)**
   - Life-threatening situations
   - People needing immediate help
   - Critical safety information

2. **URGENT (High Priority)**
   - Evacuation route problems
   - Missing persons reports
   - Equipment failures affecting safety

3. **IMPORTANT (Medium Priority)**
   - Status updates from staff
   - Coordination between teams
   - Resource needs

4. **ROUTINE (Lowest Priority - SUSPENDED during emergencies)**
   - General questions
   - Non-emergency coordination
   - Social communication

## üìû External Communication Protocols

### When to Call 911

<Info>
**In the game:** Follow roleplay protocols. **In real life:** Call 911 for actual emergencies.
</Info>

**Call 911 when:**
- Someone is seriously injured
- There is immediate danger to life
- You witness a crime in progress
- There is a fire or explosion
- Someone is having a medical emergency

**Do NOT call 911 for:**
- Information about ongoing situations
- Non-emergency questions
- Situations already being handled by staff

### Parent/Guardian Communication

**Official Channels Only:**
- School administration handles all parent communication
- Information released through official school channels
- Parents should NOT be contacted by individual staff during emergencies

**What Parents Will Receive:**
- Initial notification of emergency
- Status updates as available
- Instructions for student pickup (if applicable)
- All-clear notification when safe

## üí° Communication Best Practices

### During Emergencies

**Be Clear and Concise:**
- Use simple, direct language
- Avoid technical jargon or codes
- Repeat important information
- Confirm understanding when possible

**Stay Calm and Professional:**
- Keep your voice steady and clear
- Don't let panic affect your communication
- Focus on facts, not speculation
- Maintain authority and confidence

**Follow the Chain of Command:**
- Report to designated personnel
- Don't bypass established procedures
- Wait for official information before acting
- Support the overall communication plan

### Information Accuracy

<Warning>
**Misinformation during emergencies can be dangerous.** Only share information you know to be true from official sources.
</Warning>

**Reliable Sources:**
- PA announcements from administration
- Direct instructions from emergency responders
- Official text/email communications
- Face-to-face instructions from known staff

**Unreliable Sources:**
- Social media posts
- Rumors from other students/staff
- Information from unknown individuals
- Speculation or assumptions
