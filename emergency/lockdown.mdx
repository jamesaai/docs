# üîí Lockdown Procedures

> **Critical Security Information:** These procedures teach real security protocols that could protect lives during actual dangerous situations.

Lockdown procedures are activated when there is a threat to school safety that requires securing all areas and restricting movement.

---

## üö® When Lockdown is Announced

### Immediate Response (First 15 seconds)

<Warning>
**IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED:** When lockdown is announced, you have seconds to respond appropriately.
</Warning>

1. **Listen carefully to the announcement:**
   - **"Lockdown, lockdown, lockdown"** = Immediate threat
   - **"Secure the building"** = Potential external threat
   - **"Shelter in place"** = Environmental hazard

2. **Stop all movement immediately:**
   - Do not continue to your destination
   - Do not leave your current area
   - Follow instructions of nearest staff member

## üè† Safe Room Procedures

### Finding a Safe Room

**Priority order for safe rooms:**
1. **Classroom with locking door** (Rating: Excellent)
2. **Office with solid door** (Rating: Very Good)  
3. **Storage room or closet** (Rating: Good)
4. **Bathroom with locking door** (Rating: Fair)
5. **Any room that can be secured** (Rating: Last resort)

### Securing Your Safe Room

1. **Lock the door immediately**
   - Use deadbolt if available
   - Engage all available locks
   - If no lock exists, barricade the door

2. **Barricade if possible**
   - Move desks, chairs, or heavy objects against door
   - Create multiple layers of obstacles
   - Leave enough room to move away from the door

3. **Control visibility**
   - Turn off lights
   - Close blinds or curtains
   - Move away from windows and doors
   - Stay out of sight from outside

### Inside the Safe Room

<Info>
**Remember:** The goal is to become invisible and inaccessible until the all-clear is given.
</Info>

4. **Position everyone safely**
   - Gather in the corner farthest from the door
   - Stay below window level
   - Keep everyone together and quiet
   - Prepare to move if necessary

5. **Maintain complete silence**
   - No talking, whispering, or movement
   - Turn off all electronic devices
   - Muffle any unavoidable sounds
   - Control breathing to stay calm

6. **Stay alert and ready**
   - Listen for official instructions
   - Watch for signs of danger
   - Be prepared to follow additional directions
   - Do not assume the threat has passed

## üéØ Different Types of Lockdown

### Standard Lockdown
- **Threat:** Potential danger in or near building
- **Response:** Secure all rooms, restrict movement
- **Duration:** Until threat is neutralized or cleared

### Soft Lockdown (Secure the Building)
- **Threat:** External threat not immediately dangerous
- **Response:** Continue activities but secure perimeter
- **Duration:** Until external threat passes

### Shelter in Place
- **Threat:** Environmental hazard (gas leak, severe weather)
- **Response:** Stay indoors, seal rooms if necessary
- **Duration:** Until environmental hazard clears

### Evacuation During Lockdown
- **Only when:** Specifically instructed by law enforcement
- **Never:** Leave safe room without official instruction
- **Remember:** Sometimes staying put is safer than moving

## üë®‚Äçüè´ Staff Responsibilities During Lockdown

### Teachers and Classroom Staff

1. **Immediate classroom security:**
   - Lock classroom door immediately
   - Turn off lights and close blinds
   - Move students away from doors/windows
   - Take attendance silently

2. **Maintain classroom control:**
   - Keep students calm and quiet
   - Be ready to give additional instructions
   - Monitor for signs of panic or medical issues
   - Prepare for possible evacuation if instructed

3. **Communication protocols:**
   - Monitor official communication channels
   - Do not use phones unless absolutely necessary
   - Be ready to relay official instructions
   - Report emergencies only if safe to do so

### Administrative Staff

1. **Coordinate with law enforcement**
2. **Monitor all communication systems**
3. **Account for all individuals in building**
4. **Manage external communications**

### Security Staff

1. **Secure all building entrances**
2. **Monitor security systems**
3. **Assist law enforcement as directed**
4. **Coordinate with administrative staff**

## üö´ What NOT to Do During Lockdown

### Critical Mistakes to Avoid

<Warning>
These actions can compromise safety during lockdown situations:
</Warning>

‚ùå **Never:**
- Leave your safe room without official clearance
- Make noise or talk unless absolutely necessary
- Use electronic devices that make sound
- Look out windows or doors to "see what's happening"
- Try to contact people outside during active lockdown
- Assume the threat is over because it's been quiet
- Open doors for anyone claiming to be police (wait for official all-clear)

‚ùå **Do not:**
- Panic or create panic in others
- Try to be a hero - follow established procedures
- Use social media to post about the situation
- Ignore staff instructions
- Leave your assigned area

## üìû Communication During Lockdown

### Official Communication Channels

1. **PA System:**
   - Primary source for instructions
   - Listen carefully to all announcements
   - Follow the most recent instructions

2. **Emergency Text/Email:**
   - Sent to staff and authorized personnel
   - Contains official updates and instructions
   - Do not share information publicly

3. **Two-Way Radios:**
   - Used by staff and security
   - Emergency communications only
   - Keep channels clear for official use

### What You Should NOT Communicate

‚ùå **Do not:**
- Post on social media during lockdown
- Call or text family members during active threat
- Share location information publicly
- Spread rumors or unconfirmed information
- Contact news media

## üîÑ Lockdown Termination

### Official All-Clear Process

<Info>
**Important:** Only official personnel can declare lockdown over. Do not leave safe rooms until given explicit clearance.
</Info>

1. **Wait for official announcement:**
   - "All clear, lockdown is lifted"
   - Announcement will be repeated multiple times
   - May include specific instructions for movement

2. **Verify the source:**
   - Official announcements come through PA system
   - May be verified by known staff members
   - Law enforcement may provide face-to-face clearance

3. **Organized exit from safe rooms:**
   - Exit slowly and carefully
   - Keep hands visible
   - Follow all instructions from officials
   - Be prepared to show identification

### Post-Lockdown Procedures

4. **Account for everyone:**
   - Report to designated assembly areas
   - Staff will take attendance
   - Report any injuries or concerns

5. **Await further instructions:**
   - May be dismissed or return to normal activities
   - Counseling support may be available
   - Debrief process will follow

## üß† Psychological Preparedness

### Staying Calm During Lockdown

- **Breathe deeply and slowly**
- **Focus on following procedures**
- **Trust in your training and preparation**
- **Support others around you**
- **Remember that these procedures work**

### Managing Fear and Anxiety

- **It's normal to feel scared**
- **Focus on what you can control**
- **Follow the procedures you've practiced**
- **Stay connected to others in your safe room**
- **Remember that help is coming**

## üéØ Special Situations

### If You're in Hallways or Common Areas

1. **Move to nearest safe room immediately**
2. **Do not return to your original destination**
3. **Help others find safe rooms if possible**
4. **Follow instructions of nearest staff member**

### If You're in Bathrooms

1. **Lock the door if possible**
2. **Move to a stall and lock it**
3. **Stay quiet and still**
4. **Do not flush or run water unnecessarily**

### If You're Outside

1. **Move away from the building quickly**
2. **Seek shelter in nearby buildings if safe**
3. **Do not attempt to enter the school building**
4. **Follow instructions from emergency responders**
