## üöÄ Getting Started

<Info>
**New Player Priority:** Focus on learning the basics before trying to be a leader or expert.
</Info>

**Before You Start:**
- Read the [Emergency Rules](/rules/emergency-rules) thoroughly
- Study the [School Layout](/player/school-layout) for exits and safe rooms
- Understand [Roleplay Guidelines](/rules/roleplay) for realistic interaction
- Join our [Discord](https://Discord.gg/firealarm) for community support

**Your First Session:**
- Observe other players and how they interact
- Find a friendly group of students to learn from
- Stay close to experienced players during emergencies
- Focus on following instructions rather than leading

**Character Roles:**
- **New Student:** Perfect for learning, asks lots of questions
- **Transfer Student:** Has some experience, eager to fit in
- **Returning Student:** Knows procedures, helps newcomers
- **Advanced Student:** Experienced, takes on leadership roles

---

## üé≠ Roleplay Excellence

**Stay Consistent:**
- Remember your character's background and personality
- React authentically to different situations
- Develop relationships with other characters over time
- Show growth and learning throughout sessions

**Realistic Reactions:**
- **Fire alarm:** Show appropriate surprise or concern
- **Lockdown:** Display serious attention to safety
- **All-clear:** Express relief and discuss the experience
- **Helping others:** Show pride in contributing to safety

**Advanced Techniques:**
- Show emotional depth during emergencies
- Build friendships through shared experiences
- Step up when others are confused or scared
- Support staff and other leaders appropriately

## üö® Emergency Response

### üî• Fire Evacuation
- Always know your nearest two exits and assembly points
- React immediately when alarms sound
- Help others who seem confused
- Follow evacuation routes efficiently
- Help organize groups at assembly points

### üîí Lockdown Procedures
- Know safe room locations throughout the building
- Secure your area immediately and efficiently
- Help others get to safety quickly and quietly
- Maintain absolute silence when required
- Be ready to follow additional instructions

---

## ü§ù Social & Communication Skills

**Building Relationships:**
- Introduce yourself to new players
- Share experiences and learn from others
- Support friends during emergency situations
- Include others in your friend group

**Effective Communication:**
- Speak clearly during emergencies
- Use simple, direct language
- Ask questions when you don't understand
- Encourage others during difficult drills
- Help translate information for confused players

---

## üè´ Navigation & Leadership

**Building Knowledge:**
- Learn all floors: entrances, classrooms, exits, safe rooms
- Understand traffic flow during evacuations
- Know alternate paths when primary routes are blocked
- Practice moving efficiently in groups

**Leadership Skills:**
- Lead by example rather than giving orders
- Help without being asked
- Stay calm to help others stay calm
- Keep groups together during evacuations
- Report problems to staff efficiently

**Teaching Others:**
- Share knowledge patiently and kindly
- Demonstrate procedures rather than just explaining
- Create informal study groups
- Celebrate others' progress and success

---

## üéÆ Advanced Tips

**Game Systems:**
- Learn chat communication during emergencies
- Understand PA announcement types
- Practice emergency reporting procedures
- Work toward long-term reputation building

**Continuous Improvement:**
- Practice procedures until they're automatic
- Ask for feedback from staff and peers
- Set both short-term and long-term goals
- Participate in community events

**Community Leadership:**
- Consistently follow all rules and procedures
- Guide new players through their first emergencies
- Help create a welcoming environment for all players
- Contribute to community knowledge and resources
