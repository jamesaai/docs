## üî• Fire Evacuation Procedures

### Immediate Response (0-15 seconds)

<Warning>
**Critical:** When you hear the fire alarm, you have seconds to start moving. Every moment of delay increases danger.
</Warning>

**Step 1: Stop and Listen**
- Stop all activity immediately
- Listen for alarm type and announcements
- Alert anyone who may not have heard the alarm
- Do not collect belongings or finish tasks

**Step 2: Assess Your Immediate Area**
- Check for fire, smoke, or heat
- Identify your nearest exit route
- Note anyone who needs assistance
- Prepare to move quickly

**Step 3: Begin Evacuation**
- Start moving toward your nearest safe exit
- Help others begin evacuation
- Close doors behind you (but don't lock them)
- Do not use elevators under any circumstances

### Evacuation Movement (15 seconds - 3 minutes)

**Step 4: Choose Your Route**
- **Primary Route:** Nearest designated exit
- **Secondary Route:** Next nearest exit if primary is blocked
- **Emergency Route:** Any safe exit if others are unavailable
- **Never:** Attempt blocked or dangerous routes

**Step 5: Move Efficiently**
- Walk quickly but do not run
- Stay to the right in hallways and stairwells
- Keep groups together when possible
- Help others without stopping traffic flow

**Step 6: Navigate Obstacles**
- If door feels hot, use alternate route
- If smoke is present, stay low to the ground
- If path is blocked, find nearest alternate exit
- If trapped, go to nearest safe room and signal for help

### Reaching Safety (3-5 minutes)

**Step 7: Exit the Building**
- Continue directly outside
- Do not stop just outside doors
- Move at least 150 feet from the building
- Help direct others away from the building

**Step 8: Report to Assembly Point**
- Go directly to your designated assembly point
- Find your group or class organization
- Report any missing persons immediately
- Stay in your assigned area

## üîí Lockdown vs. Evacuation Decisions

### When to Evacuate During Lockdown

<Info>
**Critical Decision:** Sometimes evacuation is safer than lockdown, but only move when specifically instructed by authorities.
</Info>

**Evacuate When:**
- Specifically instructed by law enforcement
- Fire alarm sounds during lockdown (fire takes priority)
- Your safe room is compromised
- Directed by emergency responders

**Stay in Lockdown When:**
- No specific evacuation order given
- Uncertain about threat location
- Safe room is secure and concealed
- Moving would expose you to danger

### Emergency Evacuation During Lockdown

**Modified Evacuation Procedures:**
- Move only when directed by authorities
- Use routes specified by law enforcement
- Stay low and move quickly
- Do not stop to help others unless specifically safe
- Go directly to law enforcement staging area

**Communication During Emergency Evacuation:**
- Follow only official instructions
- Do not use personal communication devices
- Listen for law enforcement commands
- Report to designated accountability areas

## ‚ôø Accessibility and Special Needs

### Mobility-Assisted Evacuations

**Wheelchair Users:**
- Use ramps and accessible exits only
- Never attempt stairs without assistance
- Designated staff will provide evacuation assistance
- Emergency evacuation chairs available in stairwells

**Evacuation Chair Procedures:**
1. Staff member retrieves evacuation chair from stairwell
2. Transfer individual to evacuation chair
3. Two staff members operate chair down stairwell
4. Continue to accessible exit and assembly point
5. Provide medical attention if needed

### Sensory Impairments

**Hearing Impaired:**
- Visual alarm systems throughout building
- Staff trained in basic sign language
- Written evacuation instructions available
- Buddy system for emergency communication

**Vision Impaired:**
- Audio alarm systems with voice instructions
- Staff assistance for navigation
- Tactile guidance along evacuation routes
- Service animals remain with individuals

### Medical Conditions

**Individuals with Medical Needs:**
- Nurse's office serves as medical staging area
- Emergency medical supplies available
- Staff trained in basic first aid
- Direct communication with emergency medical services

**Medication Dependencies:**
- Emergency medication supplies in nurse's office
- Individual emergency action plans on file
- Staff aware of critical medical needs
- Priority evacuation for life-threatening conditions

## üå¶Ô∏è Weather-Related Evacuations

### Severe Weather Procedures

**Tornado Warnings:**
- Move to lowest floor interior rooms
- Stay away from windows and large roof areas
- Cafeteria and gymnasium serve as shelter areas
- Remain in protective position until all-clear

**Severe Thunderstorms:**
- Continue with fire evacuation if alarm sounds
- Assembly points may be moved indoors
- Gymnasium and cafeteria serve as alternate assembly areas
- Wait for weather to clear before normal dismissal

### Hazardous Materials

**Chemical Spills or Gas Leaks:**
- Evacuate upwind from hazard source
- Assembly points may be relocated
- Do not re-enter building until cleared by authorities
- Special decontamination procedures may be required

**Air Quality Issues:**
- Shelter in place procedures may override evacuation
- Seal rooms and turn off ventilation systems
- Use designated shelter areas with filtered air
- Wait for official all-clear from environmental authorities

## üöë Medical Emergency Evacuations

### Injured Person Evacuation

**Minor Injuries:**
- Evacuate with assistance if able to walk
- Report to medical station at assembly point
- Staff provides basic first aid
- Parents/guardians notified as appropriate

**Serious Injuries:**
- Do not move unless immediate danger present
- Emergency medical services called immediately
- Medical personnel handle all movement
- Evacuation routes kept clear for emergency vehicles

### Medical Emergency During Evacuation

**If Someone Collapses:**
1. Do not stop the evacuation flow
2. Alert nearest staff member immediately
3. Continue your evacuation
4. Staff will handle medical emergency
5. Keep evacuation routes clear

**If You Have Medical Emergency:**
- Alert someone immediately
- Move to side of evacuation route if possible
- Wait for staff assistance
- Do not attempt to continue alone if unsafe

## üéØ Evacuation Best Practices

### Before Emergencies

**Preparation:**
- Know all evacuation routes from areas you frequent
- Understand assembly point locations and procedures
- Practice evacuation routes during calm periods
- Keep evacuation routes clear of personal belongings

**Mental Preparation:**
- Visualize evacuation procedures regularly
- Practice staying calm under pressure
- Know how to help others during evacuations
- Understand your role in different emergency types

### During Evacuations

**Personal Actions:**
- React immediately to evacuation signals
- Help others without stopping your own evacuation
- Follow designated routes even if others seem faster
- Report problems to staff but keep moving

**Group Coordination:**
- Stay with your assigned group when possible
- Help keep groups together during movement
- Assist group leaders with accountability
- Support group members who need help

### After Evacuations

**Assembly Point Procedures:**
- Report to designated area immediately
- Participate in accountability procedures
- Stay in assigned area until dismissed
- Report any injuries or problems to staff

**Post-Evacuation Activities:**
- Participate in debriefing sessions
- Provide feedback on evacuation effectiveness
- Learn from any problems encountered
- Practice improved procedures for next time

## üí° Advanced Evacuation Techniques

### Leadership During Evacuations

**Taking Initiative:**
- Help direct others to proper exits
- Assist with traffic flow in bottleneck areas
- Support staff with group management
- Encourage calm, efficient movement

**Problem Solving:**
- Identify alternate routes when needed
- Help resolve minor evacuation obstacles
- Coordinate with other student leaders
- Communicate problems to staff efficiently

### Emergency Decision Making

**Route Selection:**
- Choose less crowded routes when safe
- Avoid routes with known obstacles
- Consider weather and environmental factors
- Adapt to changing conditions quickly

**Helping Others:**
- Assist without compromising your own safety
- Know when to get staff help instead
- Balance helping others with evacuation speed
- Prioritize life-threatening situations
