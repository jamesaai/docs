## üö™ Primary Exit Locations

### First Floor Exits

**Main Entrance (Exit A)**
- **Location:** Front of building, central lobby
- **Capacity:** High - handles 200+ people efficiently
- **Assembly Point:** Front parking lot (Assembly Point Alpha)
- **Best For:** General evacuations, main floor occupants
- **Accessibility:** Fully accessible, automatic doors

**Cafeteria Exit (Exit B)**
- **Location:** East side of building, cafeteria
- **Capacity:** Medium - handles 100+ people
- **Assembly Point:** Side parking area (Assembly Point Bravo)
- **Best For:** Cafeteria occupants, east wing evacuations
- **Features:** Double doors, direct outdoor access

**Gymnasium Exit (Exit C)**
- **Location:** West side of building, gymnasium
- **Capacity:** High - handles 150+ people
- **Assembly Point:** Athletic field (Assembly Point Charlie)
- **Best For:** Gym activities, west wing evacuations
- **Features:** Large doors, direct field access

### Second Floor Exits

**Main Stairwell (Exits D, E, F)**
- **Location:** Three main stairwells throughout building
- **Capacity:** Medium - 75-100 people per stairwell
- **Assembly Points:** Varies by stairwell location
- **Best For:** Classroom evacuations, general second floor
- **Features:** Emergency lighting, handrails, non-slip surfaces

**Library Emergency Exit (Exit G)**
- **Location:** North side of building, library
- **Capacity:** Low - 50 people maximum
- **Assembly Point:** North parking area
- **Best For:** Library occupants, north wing emergencies
- **Features:** Direct external staircase

### Third Floor Exits

**Stairwell Access Only**
- **Primary Route:** Main stairwells to first floor exits
- **Emergency Route:** Fire escape ladders (staff-assisted only)
- **Capacity:** Depends on stairwell traffic flow
- **Assembly Points:** Same as first floor destinations
- **Special Considerations:** Longest evacuation time, requires traffic management

- **Capacity:** 500+ people

- **Features:** Paved area, secondary vehicle access

## üö∂ Navigation Tips

### Traffic Flow Patterns

**Peak Evacuation Times:**
- **Class Changes:** Hallways crowded, use alternate routes
- **Lunch Period:** Cafeteria area congested, plan accordingly
- **Assembly Times:** Auditorium area busy, know alternate paths
- **Emergency Drills:** All areas active, follow designated routes

**Efficient Movement Strategies:**
- **Stay Right:** Keep right in hallways and stairwells
- **No Running:** Walk quickly but safely
- **Help Others:** Assist those who need help without stopping traffic
- **Follow Signs:** Emergency exit signs are placed strategically

### Accessibility Considerations

**Mobility Assistance Routes:**
- **Elevator Access:** For daily use only - never during fire alarms
- **Ramp Locations:** First floor ramps at main entrance and cafeteria
- **Wide Doorways:** All primary exits accommodate wheelchairs
- **Assistance Areas:** Designated areas for those needing help

**Special Assistance Procedures:**
- **Buddy System:** Pair system for those needing evacuation help
- **Staff Assignments:** Specific staff assigned to assist individuals
- **Equipment Locations:** Evacuation chairs stored in stairwell areas
- **Communication:** Special signals for hearing-impaired individuals

**Virtual Tours:**

- Practice evacuation routes during different scenarios
- Time yourself on different routes

- Create mental maps of each floor

- Practice describing routes to others
- Use landmarks to remember key locations

### Emergency Preparedness

**Route Planning:**
- Know your two nearest exits from any location
- Identify safe rooms within 30 seconds of any position
- Understand which routes work best during different emergencies
- Practice moving between floors efficiently

**Situational Awareness:**
- Always know your current location in the building
- Identify other people in your immediate area
- Notice any obstacles or hazards in evacuation routes
- Stay alert to changing conditions during emergencies
