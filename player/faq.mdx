## üö® Emergency Procedures

### Q: What's the difference between fire alarms and lockdown procedures?

**A:** Key differences:
- **Fire Alarms:** Evacuate the building immediately, go to assembly points
- **Lockdowns:** Secure your location, hide, stay silent until all-clear
- **Fire takes priority:** If fire alarm sounds during lockdown, evacuate

### Q: What if I'm in the bathroom when an emergency starts?

**A:** Emergency bathroom procedures:
- **Fire alarm:** Exit bathroom immediately, join nearest evacuation route
- **Lockdown:** Lock bathroom door if possible, stay in stall, remain quiet
- **Don't flush or run water** unnecessarily during lockdown

### Q: What if I get separated from my group during evacuation?

**A:** If separated during evacuation:
1. Continue to nearest safe exit - don't go back to find your group
2. Report to the assembly point your exit leads to
3. Tell staff you were separated from your original group
4. Staff will help reunite you and account for everyone

### Q: Can I help someone who's injured during an emergency?

**A:** Helping injured people:
- **Minor help:** Guide someone who's confused or scared
- **Don't stop traffic:** Help without blocking evacuation routes
- **Get staff for serious injuries:** Don't try to move seriously injured people
- **Your safety first:** Don't put yourself in danger to help others

## üìã Rules and Conduct

### Q: What happens if I break a rule accidentally?

**A:** Accidental rule violations:
- **Minor mistakes:** Usually result in friendly reminders
- **Show you're learning:** Accept feedback positively
- **Ask questions:** If you're unsure about rules, ask staff
- **Most violations are learning opportunities,** not punishments

### Q: Can I appeal a consequence I think was unfair?

**A:** Yes, you have the right to appeal:
1. Contact staff within 24 hours
2. Explain your perspective respectfully
3. Acknowledge any mistakes you made
4. Appeals are reviewed by senior staff
5. Accept the final decision gracefully

### Q: What's considered "trolling" during emergencies?

**A:** Emergency trolling includes:
- Making jokes during fire alarms or lockdowns
- Intentionally not following procedures
- Disrupting others' emergency responses
- Treating emergencies like a game instead of serious training
- **This results in immediate consequences**

## üè´ Building and Navigation

### Q: How do I learn all the exits and safe rooms?

**A:** Building mastery tips:
1. Take virtual tours during calm periods
2. Practice walking different evacuation routes
3. Study the [School Layout](/player/school-layout) guide
4. Time yourself on different routes
5. Ask experienced players to show you around

### Q: What if my usual exit is blocked during an emergency?

**A:** Blocked exit procedures:
1. **Don't panic** - buildings have multiple exits
2. **Use your secondary route** you should have planned
3. **Follow others** who know alternate routes
4. **Never try to force through** a blocked or dangerous exit
5. **Go to nearest safe room** if all exits seem blocked

### Q: Are there different rules for different floors?

**A:** Floor-specific considerations:
- **First floor:** Fastest evacuation, most exit options
- **Second floor:** Standard procedures, good safe room options
- **Third floor:** Longest evacuation time, excellent lockdown security
- **Basic rules are the same** for all floors
